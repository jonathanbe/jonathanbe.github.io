Beef Wellington is a preparation of filet steak coated with pâté (often pâté de foie gras) and duxelles, which is then wrapped in puff pastry and baked. Some recipes include wrapping the coated meat in a crêpe to retain the moisture and prevent it from making the pastry soggy. A whole tenderloin may be wrapped and baked, and then sliced for serving, or the tenderloin may be sliced into individual portions prior to wrapping and baking. Many spices may be added to enhance the flavour; some examples are allspice and ginger.
Wikipedia

ingredients

  • 5lb (2.25 Kg) Whole Fillet of Beef

  • 1 Shallot - finely chopped

  • 3 tbsp (45 ml) of butter

  • 1 lb (450 g) mushrooms - finely chopped

  • A Grating of Fresh Nutmeg

  • 2 Large Sheets of Puff Pastry

  • Salt & Freshly Ground Pepper

  • 8 oz (225 g) Prepared Pate de Foie

  • 1 Egg Yolk Mixed With 1 tbsp (15 ml) of Water

instructions

  1. Place the fillet on a wire rack in a roasting pan

  2. Roast in a preheated 425F (220C) oven until it reaches an internal temperature of 120F (48C)

  3. Remove from the oven and let the meat return to room temperature after 20 minutes

  4. Saute the shallot in the butter until tender but not browned (~5 mins.)

  5. Add chopped mushrooms & saute over high heat (stir frequently)

    *Stir until nearly all of the moisture has evaporated - making the mixture resemble a coarse paste

  6. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper, and allow to cool to room temperature

    7. Assembly

  7. Roll out the sheets of puff pastry until they are about 2in. / 5cm longer &smp; wider than the fillet

  8. Place the fillet in the center of one of the sheets of puff pastry, & spread the pate de foie in a thin layer over the top & sides of the beef

  9. Top with the mushroom mixture

  10. Brush the edges of the pastry with water

  11. Place the second sheet of puff pastry over the top

  12. Trim and seal the edges, crimping with your fingers or the tines of a fork

  13. ecorate the top of the log with any remaining puff pastry cut into decorative shapes and brush with the egg yolk mixture

  14. Bake in a preheated 425F (220C) oven for 10 minutes

  15. Reduce the heat to 375F (190C) and bake until the crust is golden brown, about 20 minutes

  16. Remove from the oven and allow to rest 10 minutes

  17. To serve, cut with a very sharp knife into slices about 3/4 inch (2 cm) thick
Estimated Calorie Count: ~5940 total / ~ 495 - 742 per serving

This recipe serves 8-12
