The dish originates from the Burgundy region (in French, Bourgogne) which is in the east of present-day France, as do many other dishes such as coq au vin, escargot, persillé ham, œufs meurette, gougères, pain d'épices, etc. It is a stew prepared with beef braised in red wine, traditionally red Burgundy, and beef broth, generally flavoured with garlic, onions and a bouquet garni, with pearl onions and mushrooms added towards the end of cooking.
Wikipedia

ingredients

  • 6oz (170g) Blanched Bacon Lardons1

  • 2tbsp (30 ml) of Vegetable Oil

  • About 4lbs (2Kg) Trimmed Beef Chuck Cut Into 2 inch (5 cm) Cubes

  • Salt & Freshly Ground Pepper

  • 2 Cups (500ml) Sliced Onions

  • 1 cup (250ml) Sliced Carrots

  • 1 Bottle (750ml) of Red Wine (Zinfandel, Chianti, etc.)

  • 2 Cups (500ml) Beef Stock

  • 1 Cup (250ml) Chopped Tomatoes

  • 1 Herb bouquet2

  • 3tbsp (45ml) All Purpose Flour Blended To A Paste With 2tbsp (30ml) of Butter

  • 1 Recipe Brown-Braised Small White Onions3

  • 1 Recipe Sauteed Mushrooms4



    • 1Blanched Lardons
  • 6oz (170g) Bacon or Salt Pork
    • Cut the bacon or salt pork into pieces 1/4 inch (5mm) square and 1inch (2.5cm) long. Drop into 2 quarts (2L) of boiling water and boil for 6-8 minutes. Drain and pat dry with paper towels.



    2Julia Child's Herb Bouquet
  • 8 Sprigs Fresh Parsley
  • 1 Large Bay (Laurel) Leaf
  • 1tsp (5ml) Dried Thyme
  • 4 Allspice Berries
  • 3 Large Cloves, Garlic (Smashed)
    • Combine all ingredients and tie together in a small piece of cheesecloth (muslin)



    3Brown-Braised Onions
  • 12-20 Small White Onions
  • 2tbsp (30ml) Butter
  • ~1 cup (~250 ml) Chicken Stock
  • 1tsp (5ml) Sugar
  • Salt & Freshly Ground Pepper To Taste
    • To peel the onions, drop them in boiling water for 1 minute. Drain and rinse under running water. Trim off the root end and the skin should slip off. Saute the onion is the butter until lightly browned. Add enough chicken stock to come halfway up the onions. Add the sugar, salt, and pepper and simmer covered until the onions are cooked through, about 20 minutes. Drain and serve. Serves 4 to 6.



    4Sauteed Mushrooms
  • 2Tbs (30ml) of Butter
  • 1Tbs (15ml) Olive or Vegetable Oil
  • 1lb (450ml) White (Parisian) Mushrooms, Quartered
  • 1 Small Shallot, Finely Chopped
  • Salt & Freshly Ground Pepper To Taste
    • Heat the butter and oil in a skillet over high heat and saute the mushrooms, tossing frequently, until they are lightly browned and the liquid is absorbed by the mushrooms and then reappears in the skillet. Add the shallot, salt, and pepper and saute for 30 seconds. Serves 4 to 6.

instructions

  1. Brown the lardons in a large skillet over moderate heat and set aside

  2. Heat the oil in the same skillet and brown the meat

  3. Season with salt and pepper

    4. *Be sure that the meat is patted dry with paper towels before adding to the skillet, and do not crowd the pan

  4. Transfer the meat & lardons to a casserole

  5. brown the onions & carrots in the same skillet

  6. Add the vegetables to the meat

  7. Deglaze the pan with some of the wine & add the resulting liquid, along with the remaining wine & beef stock to the casserole

  8. Add the tomatoes & herb bouquet - simmer covered (either on low heat on the stove or in a 325F (165C) oven, until the meat is tender, about 2 hours)

  9. Drain through a colander set over a bowl and return the meat to the casserole

  10. Press the remaining residue in the colander to extract as much liquid as possible

  11. Discard the solids & remove the grease from the surface of the resulting liquid

  12. Heat the liquid in a saucepan & reduce it to about 3 cups (750ml)

  13. Whisk in the beurre manie (flour & butter mixture) - simmer until the sauce has thickened

  14. Adjust the seasoning with salt & pepper - pour over the meat in the casserole

  15. Fold in the onions and mushrooms

    16. The dish may be made ahead of time & refrigerated at this point.

    To serve, bring to a simmer over moderate heat
