Tiramisu (from Italian, spelled tiramisù [tiramiˈsu*], meaning "pick me up" "cheer me up"[1] or "lift me up") is a popular coffee-flavored Italian dessert. It is made of lady’s fingers (Italian: Savoiardi, [savoˈjardi]) dipped in coffee, layered with a whipped mixture of eggs, sugar, and mascarpone cheese, flavoured with cocoa. The recipe has been adapted into many varieties of cakes and other desserts.[2] Its origins are often disputed among Italian regions such as Veneto, Friuli Venezia Giulia, Piedmont, and others.
Wikipedia

ingredients

  • 3 Eggs - Separated

  • 1/2 cup (125ml) Sugar

  • 8oz (225g) Mascarpone

  • About 24 Italian Savoiardi or Ladyfingers

  • 1/4 cup (60ml) Espresso Coffee

  • 1 tbsp (15ml) Brandy or Sherry

  • 1oz (28g) Bittersweet Chocolate - Grated

instructions

  1. Beat the egg whites until soft peaks form

  2. Beat the egg yolks & sugar until they are creamy & light yellow - in a different bowl

  3. Mix in the mascarpone, then fold in the egg whites

  4. Add the mascarpone, and then fold in the egg whites

  5. Add the coffee & brandy/sherry in a small bowl and

  6. Use a pastry brush to soak the ladyfingers with half the mixture

  7. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, and sprinkle with half the grated chocolate

  8. Do this again with another layer of ladyfingers brushed with the remaining liquid

  9. Top with the remaining mascarpone mixture

  10. Save the remaining grated chocolate for a last minute garnish

  11. Cover & Refrigerate for at least 3 hours

  12. Serve cold or at room temperature

  13. When serving, apply remaining grated chocolate
Estimated Calorie Count: 2090 total / ~261 - 348 per serving

This recipe serves 6-8
