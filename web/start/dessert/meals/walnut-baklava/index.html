Baklava (/ˈbɑːkləvɑː/, /bɑːkləˈvɑː/, or /bəˈklɑːvə/; Ottoman Turkish: باقلوا [bɑːklɑvɑː]) is a rich, sweet pastry made of layers of filo filled with chopped nuts and sweetened and held together with syrup or honey. It is characteristic of the cuisines of the former Ottoman Empire, and is also found in Central and Southwest Asia.
Wikipedia

ingredients

  • 4 cups of Chopped Walnuts

  • 1/2 cup of Ssugar

  • 1 tbsp Ground Cinnamon

  • 1 1/4 cups of Melted Butter

  • 1 1/2 lbs (700g) of Phyllo

    • Syrup
  • 4 cups of Sugar
  • 3 cups of Water
  • 1 1/2 cups Hone
  • 1 Cinnamon Stick
  • 5 - 6 Whole Cloves
    • Ccombine the sugar & water in a large pot. Boil this for 5 minutes. Next, add the honey, cinnamon stick and cloves, and boil for 5 minutes more. Pour the hot syrup over the cooled baklava. Do not pour over a hot baklava, it will turn soggy. Cool then cut through the bottom layer of phyllo following the previous cuts.

instructions

  1. Mix the walnuts,sugar, and cinnamon

  2. Take a pan the size of your phyllo sheets and brush it with melted butter

  3. Use 4 sheets of phyllo for the bottom layer - brush each one with butter before adding the next - top sheet without butter

  4. Add some walnut mixture

  5. Cover with an unbuttered sheet of phyllo

  6. Add more walnuts

  7. Cover with a buttered sheet, an unbuttered sheet, and another sprinkling of walnuts. Repeat until the ingredients are used up, reserving 5 sheet of phyllo for the top layer

  8. Brush each of the top 5 sheets with a fair amount of butter, pour the remaining butter over the top of the last sheet

  9. Cut the baklava into a diamond pattern (as is tradition) - do not cut through the bottom layer

  10. Sprinkle the top with water (using your fingertips)

  11. Bake in an oven - 250F (120C) for roughly 1 hour

  12. Remove and let it cool
